My strongest product lessons did not come from a roadmap. They came from customers who were under pressure. In healthcare automation, a single failed integration can stall patient care, billing, or scheduling. When you see how much a broken workflow can cost, you start respecting every detail.

As an engineer, I built integration pipelines and monitoring systems to prevent failures. As a Technical Account Manager, I now translate customer pain into crisp stories that the product team can act on. The hard part is not the technology—it is the narrative.

Here is the approach I use:

- **Name the moment of failure**: what breaks first for the user?
- **Define the smallest fix that restores trust**: what can we ship fastest that changes the outcome?
- **Choose the metric**: what proves this is solved?

A real example: a customer’s scheduling system kept dropping requests at peak load. The team was tempted to rebuild the entire integration. Instead, we added clear retry logic, queue visibility, and alerting. The impact was immediate. We did not just reduce errors—we reduced anxiety.

> "The best fix is the one that makes the user feel safe again."

Product clarity is the discipline of cutting through noise. If you can explain the problem in one page, you can usually solve it in two sprints. That is the standard I hold myself to.
